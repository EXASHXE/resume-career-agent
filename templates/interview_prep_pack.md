## Behavioral Questions

### Leadership & Ownership

1. **Tell me about a time you drove a technical decision that the team disagreed with.**
   - Framework: Context → Disagreement → Your reasoning → How you handled pushback → Outcome → What you learned

2. **Describe a project where you had to say no to a stakeholder request.**
   - Framework: Request → Constraint/trade-off → Your communication → Alternative proposed → Result

3. **Tell me about a time you mentored someone or improved team practices.**
   - Framework: Problem observed → Your action → Adoption → Measurable improvement

### Failure & Learning

4. **Describe a technical failure you were responsible for and how you handled it.**
   - Framework: What failed → Root cause → Immediate response → Long-term fix → Lessons

5. **Tell me about a project that didn't ship. What happened?**
   - Framework: Original goal → Blockers → Your actions → What you learned about scoping/risk

### Collaboration

6. **Describe working with a difficult cross-team dependency.**
   - Framework: Dependency → Conflict → Your communication strategy → Resolution → Working relationship

## Risk Questions (Be Prepared)

1. **What gaps do you see in your background for this role?**
   - Be honest. Acknowledge the gap. Describe your plan to close it. Show related experience.

2. **Why are you leaving / Why did you leave {{current_company}}?**
   - Framework: Positive framing of what you learned → What you're looking for next → Why this role

3. **What's your expected compensation?**
   - Know your range beforehand. Reference market data. Can defer to later stages.

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

### About the Role

1. What does success look like in the first 6 months?
2. What's the most challenging problem the team is facing right now?
3. How does this role interact with other engineering teams?

### About the Team

4. What's the team composition (seniority, size, locations)?
5. How does the team handle design reviews and technical decisions?
6. What's the on-call / operational responsibility look like?

### About the Company

7. How does engineering prioritize technical debt vs. feature work?
8. What's the career growth framework for engineers here?
9. How does the company think about AI / LLM adoption in engineering workflows?

## English Interview Practice (if interviewing in English)

### Key phrases to practice

- "The core problem we were solving was..."
- "My specific contribution was..."
- "The trade-off we made was..."
- "We measured success by..."
- "If I were to do it again, I would..."

### Common pitfalls

- Starting every sentence with "So..."
- Over-explaining without being asked
- Using "we" for everything — know when to say "I"
- Not knowing your numbers — memorize your key metrics
- Getting defensive about gaps — acknowledge and redirect to strengths
